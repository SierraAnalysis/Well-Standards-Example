The uniformity coefficient is a ratio that describes the variation in grain size of granular aquifer material. It is defined as the ratio of the particle size of a material at which 60 percent of the particles are finer and 40 percent are coarser (called D40) to the "effective" grain size (i.e., the particle size of the material at which ten percent of the particles are finer and 90 percent are coarser (D90). The value of the uniformity coefficient for a material of one grain size is unity; for a heterogenouus sand it might be 30.
